1.1 The Presidential MSME (Micro Small Medium Enterprise) Awards conducted by the Department of Small Business Development (DSBD also referred to as the Department) has appointed an organising committee to plan, coordinate and execute the Awards.
The Awards are aligned with the National Integrated Small Enterprise Development (NISED) Strategic Framework, recognising outstanding MSMEs, Co-operatives, Startups, and Ecosystem enablers.

1.2. Applications open on 6 July 2026 and close on 27 July 2026

Any organisation can apply under the following categories via the website https://msmeawards.org/

MSME Categories
  • Youth-Led MSME of the Year
  • Women-Led MSME of the Year
  • MSME Owned by Persons with Disabilities of the Year
  • MSME in the Cooperative sector of the Year
  • Creative Industries MSME of the Year
Startup Competition Categories
  • GreenTech Startup of the Year
  • Agri-Tech Startup of the Year
  • Edtech Startup of the Year
  • Health Tech Startup of the Year
  • Fintech Startup of the Year

2.1 Definitions, descriptions and eligibility criteria for each of the award categories can be found on this link https://msmeawards.org/

2.2 Applications are not open to (i) any director, member, partner, employee, agent of or consultant of the Department or of the advertising or promotion agencies or anyone associated to the Department and its entities, and (ii) such prohibition extends to any spouse, life partner, immediate family members or business partners or any associate of persons listed in (i), and (iii) sponsors who provide financial or non-financial contributions to the awards.

2.3 General Eligibility Criteria
  • 2.3.1 MSMEs must be legally registered through the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC) or as cooperatives operating in South Africa.
  • 2.3.2 MSMEs must be in operation for not more than 5 years.
  • 2.3.3 MSMEs must demonstrate economic and/or social impact.
  • 2.3.4 MSMEs must comply with tax, labour, and all relevant laws and regulations.
  • 2.3.5 MSMEs must employ between two (2) to two hundred and fifty (250) people.
  • 2.3.6 MSMEs must have annual revenue of more than Ten (10) Million Rands.
2.4 Startup Competition Eligibility Criteria
  • 2.4.1 The Startup must be located and operating in a G20 country.
  • 2.4.2 Must create job opportunities, alleviate poverty, and contribute to economic growth.
  • 2.4.3 Must detail their technology innovation in the business profile.
  • 2.4.4 Must have valid country-specific business compliance documents including: (i) official business registration letter reflecting business and directors' names, (ii) directors' identification copies, (iii) tax compliant status or letter, (iv) BBBEE certificate or similar country-specific documents.
2.4 Limitations on number of entries per category
  • 2.4.1 Up to a maximum of two (2) applications per MSME will be permitted.
  • 2.4.2 Each category application must be submitted separately.
2.5 Documentation Requirements
  • 2.5.1 MSMEs must provide a motivational letter in written format of maximum 250 words.
  • 2.5.2 Startups must provide a business profile presentation of a maximum of 10 pages and a motivational letter in written format of maximum 250 words.

2.6 Applicants will be notified by email of their application, upon which they will be required to submit supporting documentation for adjudication purposes.

2.7 All correspondence regarding entry/application will be directed to the person named on the application form. Please ensure that this person is the most relevant contact; and

2.8 There is no applicable fee payable to submit an application for the Awards.

3.1 Initial Selection: Bedrock AI, powered by Amazon, will review all applications to shortlist eligible candidates.

3.2 National-Level Pitch Competitions: Selected candidates will participate in national-level adjudication based on competition criteria.. The adjudication committee will select the top three winners per category.

3.3 Provincial-Level Pitch Competitions: An independent auditing firm will verify the results and determine the winner and runners-up. The final results will be announced at the Presidential MSME Awards 2026 in Johannesburg.

3.2 National-Level Competition: Selected candidates will participate in national-level adjudication based on competition criteria.. The adjudication committee will select the top three winners per category.

3.3 Final Audit and Announcement: An independent auditing firm will verify the results and determine the winner and runners-up. The final results will be announced at the Presidential MSME Awards 2026 in Johannesburg.

National Awards Invitation: All provincial winners will be invited to the national awards ceremony.

Finality of Decisions: The decisions of the adjudication committee and auditing firm are final, and no further correspondence or appeals will be entertained.

Winners: General Award Categories

  • Winners: Each of the five (5) category winners will receive a cash prize of R200,000.
  • 1st Runners-Up: Each 1st runner-up will receive a cash prize of R100,000.
  • 2nd Runners-Up: Each 2nd runner-up will receive a cash prize of R60,000.
  • Additional Recognition: All runners-up and category winners will receive a certificate and a trophy.

MSME of the Year Award

  • Overall Winner: The MSME of the Year will receive an additional cash prize of R1,200,000 (bringing their total winnings to R1,400,000 including their category win).
  • 1st Runner-Up: Will receive an additional cash prize of R800,000 (bringing their total to R1,000,000 including their category win).
  • 2nd Runner-Up: Will receive an additional cash prize of R600,000 (bringing their total to R800,000 including their category win).

Additional Prizes

  • All runners-up and category winners will receive a certificate and a trophy.

Non-Transferability

  • The awards and their associated prizes are not transferable, negotiable, or exchangeable for cash.

Startup Competition Prizes

  • GreenTech Startup Winner: Cash prize of R500,000 ($28,000).
  • AgriTech Startup Winner: Cash prize of R500,000 ($28,000).
  • EdTech Startup Winner: Cash prize of R500,000 ($28,000).
  • HealthTech Startup Winner: Cash prize of R500,000 ($28,000).
  • FinTech Startup Winner: Cash prize of R500,000 ($28,000).
  • Additional Recognition: All startup category winners will receive a certificate and a trophy.

Non-Transferability: The awards and their associated prizes are not transferable, negotiable, or exchangeable for cash.
